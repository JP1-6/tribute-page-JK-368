Tyler, The Creator

Rapper, Musician, Singer, Producer, Actor, Fashion Icon, Innovator

known for his bold creativity, offbeat humor, and genre-blending music. He first gained fame as the leader of the alternative hip-hop collective Odd Future and later built a successful solo career with multiple critically acclaimed albums.

History of Tyler Albums

Bastard (2009)

Tyler's raw and controversial debut mixtape introduced his dark, rebellious style and shock-value lyricism.

Goblin (2011)

A darker, chaotic album where Tyler continues therapy sessions with his alter ego, Dr. TC.

Wolf (2013)

A more refined and melodic project, showing growth in production and storytelling.

Cherry Bomb (2015)

A chaotic, genre-blending experiment that swings between noisy distortion and tender melodies.

Flower Boy (2017)

A coming-of-age record with lush, jazzy production and vulnerable lyrics about love, loneliness, and self-discovery.

IGOR (2019)

A concept album about heartbreak told through synth-heavy, genre-blending soundscapes and Tyler’s evolving musical vision.

Call Me If You Get Lost (2021)

A globe-trotting, luxurious album that mixes braggadocio with personal reflection, hosted by DJ Drama.

Chromakopia (2024)

A genre-blending exploration of identity, fame, and personal growth, narrated by Tyler's mother and featuring introspective themes.
